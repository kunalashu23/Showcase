Reimagining the FICO Credit Score Experience

Transforming credit score reporting from a static number display into a personalized financial coaching platform that helps users understand, improve, and actively manage their credit health.

Client

PenFed Federal Credit Union

Duration

6 months

Role

UX Researcher & Product Strategist

Methods

Behavioral Research · Workshops · Competitor Analysis

78%

of users viewed their credit score but didn't understand how to improve it

"I check my score every month and it goes up or down, but I have no idea why. I wish it would just tell me what I'm doing wrong and how to fix it."
— Credit union member, behavioral research session

Key Findings

  • Score Without Context: Users viewed credit scores as static numbers without understanding the factors affecting them or why scores changed month-to-month.
  • Generic Recommendations: Existing guidance like "Improve your credit utilization" was too vague to translate into actionable steps for most users.
  • Financial Terminology Barrier: Terms like "utilization ratio," "credit mix," and "hard inquiries" created confusion and disengaged users from taking action.
  • Lack of Motivation: Without visible progress tracking or predicted outcomes, users had no incentive to change financial behaviors consistently.
  • Missed Educational Opportunities: Financial literacy content existed separately from the score experience, preventing contextual learning moments.

Problems → Solutions

Problem

Score displayed without explanation of changes

Users saw their score increase or decrease but had no visibility into which financial behaviors caused the change.

Solution

Score change timeline with factor attribution

Show month-over-month changes with specific factors highlighted: "Your score increased by 12 points because you paid down $2,400 in credit card debt."

Problem

Generic advice that wasn't actionable

"Lower your credit utilization" provided no specific guidance on how much to pay or which accounts to prioritize.

Solution

Personalized action plans with predicted impact

"Paying $500 toward Card A could improve your score by 8-12 points. This card has the highest impact because your utilization is at 82%."

Problem

Financial education separated from score

Educational content lived in a different section. Users never discovered it when viewing their scores.

Solution

Contextual micro-learning embedded in experience

Inline tooltips, visual diagrams, and "Learn why this matters" links placed exactly where users need them most.

Research Approach

  • Behavioral research analyzing credit monitoring habits and financial decision-making patterns
  • User psychology mapping exploring financial anxiety, motivation triggers, and behavioral barriers
  • Discovery workshops with cross-functional teams to ideate solutions and prioritize opportunities
  • Competitor analysis evaluating 12+ credit score and banking experiences for gaps and opportunities
  • Information architecture redesign restructuring the experience from score reporting to financial coaching
"This is the first time someone has explained credit scores in a way I actually understand. I always felt stupid asking what these terms meant."
— User testing participant, validation session

5 Actions Driven by UX Principles

  • Transparency + Trust Building

    Created score factor breakdown with clear explanations

    Transformed opaque credit scores into transparent calculations. Users now see exactly how payment history (35%), credit utilization (30%), credit age (15%), credit mix (10%), and new credit (10%) contribute to their score — building trust through transparency.

  • Behavioral Economics + Goal Gradient Effect

    Designed progress tracking with predicted score improvements

    Introduced visual progress bars and score forecasting: "If you continue making on-time payments, your score could reach 720 in 4 months." Research shows progress visualization increases motivation and follow-through on financial goals.

  • Personalization + Behavioral Nudges

    Built recommendation engine analyzing individual financial patterns

    Instead of generic advice, system analyzes user's specific credit profile and suggests 2-3 highest-impact actions personalized to their situation. Includes predicted score impact for each recommendation to drive prioritization.

  • Just-in-Time Learning + Cognitive Load

    Embedded contextual education throughout the experience

    Rather than overwhelming users with financial literacy content upfront, we introduced micro-learning moments contextually. Tooltips, visual diagrams, and "Why this matters" explanations appear exactly when users need them, reducing cognitive load.

  • Loss Aversion + Feedback Loops

    Implemented predictive impact messaging for financial decisions

    Before users make credit-impacting decisions (new loan applications, closing accounts), system shows predicted score impact: "Opening this card may temporarily lower your score by 5-10 points due to hard inquiry." Leverages loss aversion to encourage informed decisions.
