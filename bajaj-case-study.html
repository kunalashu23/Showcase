Bajaj Finserv Moderation Research

Pan-India ethnographic moderation research for Bajaj Finserv — 150+ in-depth interviews, rapid prototype testing, behavioral insights synthesis, and comprehensive research across 15 longitudinal studies conducted on-field, remote, and through hybrid methodologies.

Project Overview

As the lead moderator and researcher for Bajaj Finserv, I conducted comprehensive pan-India research studies combining ethnographic field work, remote moderation, and rapid prototype testing to understand customer behavior, mindsets, and financial decision-making patterns across diverse user segments. The research spanned 15 distinct studies, involving 150+ in-depth individual interviews (IDI), each lasting 60–70 minutes, totaling approximately 150–175 hours of primary research sessions.

Research Methodology

The research employed a multi-method approach combining ethnographic immersion, moderation research, and rapid experimentation to capture both behavioral patterns and attitudinal insights from Bajaj Finserv customers and prospects.

Ethnographic Field Research

On-Ground IDIs

Conducted in-depth individual interviews in participants' natural environments — homes, workplaces, and community settings — to understand financial behaviors, pain points, and decision-making contexts. Observed real-life interactions with financial products and documented behavioral patterns through field notes and visual documentation.

Remote Moderation

Virtual Sessions

Facilitated remote moderation sessions using video conferencing platforms to reach participants across different geographies and demographics. Employed screen-sharing, co-browsing, and digital diary studies to capture user interactions with Bajaj Finserv's digital products and services in real-time.

Rapid Prototyping

Concept & Prototype Testing

Conducted rapid prototype testing sessions to validate design concepts, new product features, and service innovations. Gathered immediate feedback on wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes through moderated usability sessions and iterative testing cycles.

Mixed Methods

Surveys & Diary Studies

Integrated quantitative surveys and longitudinal diary studies to complement qualitative insights. Tracked behavioral changes over time, captured contextual data, and validated findings through triangulated research methods for comprehensive understanding.

Research Process

End-to-End Research Synthesis

1

Study Design & Planning

Defined research objectives, screener criteria, and discussion guides tailored to each study's goals. Coordinated recruitment across diverse customer segments, ensuring representative sampling for pan-India coverage.

2

Field Research & Moderation

Conducted 150+ in-depth interviews (60–70 min each) through on-field visits and remote sessions. Moderated discussions exploring customer mindsets, behaviors, financial attitudes, product perceptions, and unmet needs. Employed ethnographic observation, contextual inquiry, and moderated dialogue to surface deep insights.

3

Visual Documentation & Data Capture

Visually documented field research through photography, video recordings, journey maps, and field notes. Captured behavioral data, user environments, and interaction patterns to provide rich contextual evidence supporting qualitative findings.

4

Insight Synthesis & Analysis

Analyzed qualitative data using thematic coding, affinity mapping, and pattern recognition. Synthesized behavioral insights, identified opportunity areas, and translated raw data into actionable findings aligned with business objectives.

5

Report Generation & Stakeholder Communication

Delivered comprehensive research reports with behavioral insights, user personas, journey maps, and strategic recommendations. Presented findings to cross-functional stakeholders through visual storytelling, workshop facilitation, and evidence-based narratives.

Research Focus Areas

Key Investigation Domains

  • Customer Mindset & Behavior: Deep understanding of financial attitudes, risk perceptions, decision-making heuristics, and behavioral patterns influencing product adoption and usage
  • Financial Needs & Pain Points: Identified unmet needs, friction points, and contextual challenges customers face in managing finances, accessing credit, and engaging with financial services
  • Product Perception & Usability: Evaluated how customers perceive and interact with Bajaj Finserv's products, uncovering usability issues, comprehension barriers, and feature expectations
  • Trust & Brand Perception: Explored trust-building factors, brand associations, and emotional drivers that influence customer loyalty and product choice in the finserv domain
  • Journey Mapping & Touchpoint Analysis: Documented end-to-end customer journeys across key financial tasks, identifying critical moments, emotional highs/lows, and opportunities for experience improvement
  • Prototype & Concept Validation: Tested new product concepts, feature ideas, and service innovations through rapid prototyping cycles, gathering iterative feedback to inform design and development

Deliverables & Impact

The research program generated actionable insights that informed product strategy, UX design, and customer experience initiatives at Bajaj Finserv. Deliverables included:

Research Scale & Investment

Session Duration: Each in-depth interview lasted 60–70 minutes, designed to allow deep exploration of financial behaviors and attitudes without participant fatigue.

Total Research Hours: Approximately 150–175 hours of primary research sessions across 150+ interviews, representing significant investment in understanding customer needs at depth.

Study Cadence: 15 distinct research studies conducted over the engagement period, each tailored to specific product lines, customer segments, or strategic questions.

Geographic Reach: Pan-India coverage across urban, semi-urban, and rural contexts, ensuring diversity in customer profiles, financial literacy levels, and product usage patterns.
