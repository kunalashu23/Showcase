Project Overview

Supported IIT Bombay's research on India's National Solar Mission off-grid programme — a multi-phase initiative aiming to deploy 2000 MW equivalent of solar off-grid systems across rural India by 2022. My role focused on ethnographic documentation, visual storytelling, and community engagement to capture adoption patterns, behavioral insights, and ground-level implementation challenges.

The National Solar Mission targeted areas with unreliable or no grid power through applications including solar home lighting systems, street lighting, power plants, pumps, lanterns, and study lamps. The programme had deployed 84+ lakh solar lamps, 5+ lakh solar pumps, 9+ lakh solar street lights, and 17+ lakh home lighting systems by the time of this research.

National Context

The Ministry of New and Renewable Energy (MNRE) launched the Off-grid Solar PV Applications Programme to provide solar-based solutions in areas with unreliable or absent grid power. The programme evolved through three phases:

Phase I
2010-13: 252.5 MW sanctioned, 117 MW installed across pilot implementations
Phase II
2014-17: 713 MW sanctioned, 345.5 MW installed with rural development focus
Phase III
2018-21: 20.09 MW sanctioned, 15.67 MW installed for government institutions

Programme Reach

By December 2022, the Off-grid programme had achieved:

  • 84,59,119 solar lamps/lanterns distributed
  • 5,00,499 solar pumps installed for irrigation and drinking water
  • 9,44,802 solar street lights under schemes including Atal Jyoti Yojana (AJAY)
  • 17,23,479 solar home lighting systems deployed
  • 217 MWp solar power plants/packs commissioned

The programme prioritized states with less than 50% household electrification (Census 2011): Assam, Bihar, Jharkhand, Odisha, Uttar Pradesh, and the North-Eastern region.

Ethnographic Documentation Methodology

My work centered on visual ethnography — documenting the lived experience of solar adoption through film, photography, and participatory storytelling. This approach captured behavioral patterns, cultural contexts, and implementation barriers that quantitative metrics alone couldn't reveal.

Key Insights Documented

Adoption Barriers & User Behavior

  • Quality perception gaps: Documented user skepticism toward solar technology reliability and lifespan concerns
  • Maintenance knowledge deficits: Captured on-the-ground challenges in troubleshooting and basic upkeep without accessible service networks
  • Cultural integration: Filmed how solar lighting transformed evening routines, education practices, and household productivity in kerosene-dependent communities
  • Gender-specific impacts: Documented how solar street lights improved women's mobility and safety after dark

Implementation Challenges

  • Last-mile distribution: Visualized logistical hurdles in remote, difficult-to-access tribal and hilly regions
  • Supplier quality variance: Captured performance inconsistencies across different solar product brands and procurement batches
  • Policy-to-practice gaps: Documented delays between programme sanctions and actual installation due to administrative bottlenecks
  • Community awareness: Filmed low awareness levels about programme eligibility and application processes in underserved blocks

Success Stories & Impact

  • Student study patterns: Documented how 8 million students benefitted with solar study lamps, enabling evening homework and exam preparation in non-electrified households
  • Livelihood enhancement: Captured stories of farmers using solar pumps for irrigation, increasing crop yields and income stability
  • Health improvements: Filmed reduction in kerosene-related indoor air pollution and respiratory issues post-solar adoption
  • Economic savings: Documented household budget shifts as families eliminated kerosene expenditure and mobile charging costs

Programme Scale Context

This ethnographic work documented a fraction of a massive national initiative. By the research period, the Off-grid programme had reached:

  • Atal Jyoti Yojana (AJAY): 2.72 lakh solar street lights installed in Phase I and II across low-electrification states, funded through MPLADS and MNRE central financial assistance
  • Solar Study Lamps: 60 lakh lamps distributed to school-going children in blocks with more than 50% kerosene-dependent households
  • Geographic coverage: Special focus on Assam, Bihar, Jharkhand, Odisha, Uttar Pradesh, NER states, J&K, Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand, island UTs, and aspirational districts
  • Integrated schemes: Standalone pumps transitioned to PM KUSUM scheme; solar home lights integrated into Saubhagya scheme under Ministry of Power

Personal Impact & Skills Developed

This project was foundational in shaping my ethnographic practice and cross-disciplinary research approach. It required balancing filmmaker, educator, and field researcher roles while navigating diverse cultural, linguistic, and geographic contexts.
