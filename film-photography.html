Practice & Approach

My film and photography practice is rooted in ethnographic methodology — long-term immersion, participatory documentation, and a commitment to centering the voices and stories of marginalized communities. Each project balances visual craft with ethical responsibility, aiming to document lived experience rather than impose external narratives.

Core Principles

  • Participatory documentation: Involving community members in framing, shooting, and storytelling decisions to ensure authentic representation
  • Long-term engagement: Building trust through sustained presence rather than extractive one-time visits
  • Context over aesthetics: Prioritizing cultural context, historical depth, and systemic understanding over surface-level visual appeal
  • Impact orientation: Creating work that serves community advocacy, policy influence, or educational purposes beyond gallery exhibition

Technical Approach

Documentary filmmaking using natural light, observational methods, and interview techniques adapted to rural and tribal contexts. Photography focused on environmental portraiture, daily life documentation, and visual ethnography. Field equipment prioritizes portability and resilience over high-end production value — work that can be carried into remote villages and operate in challenging conditions.

Collaborations

Worked with academic institutions (IIT Bombay, Dhirubhai Ambani University, Boston College School of Social Work), NGOs (AIM for SEWA, FWWB), government ministries (Ministry of New and Renewable Energy), and community-based organizations to document social impact, development interventions, and cultural preservation initiatives.
