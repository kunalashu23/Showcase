Strategic Workforce Planning Platform

Transforming a complex workforce planning ecosystem spanning demand planning, supply forecasting, gap analysis, and action planning into a role-driven decision platform supporting VPs, directors, HR teams, and workforce planning partners across the World Bank.

Client

World Bank

Duration

Multi-phase initiative

Role

UX Researcher & Strategist

Methods

Service Design · IA · Design Systems

5 Stages

Workforce planning ecosystem spanning Demand Planning → Supply Forecasting → Gap Analysis → Action Planning → Implementation Monitoring

"I'm responsible for demand planning but I have no visibility into what HR is forecasting for supply. By the time we realize there's a gap, it's too late to create an action plan."
— Director, Workforce Planning Research

Key Findings

  • Process-Based Planning Broken Across Systems: Workforce planning required 5 sequential stages (Demand → Supply → Gap Analysis → Action → Monitoring) but each stage lived in different tools, breaking decision continuity.
  • Role Confusion and Duplicate Ownership: VPs, Directors, HR CSMs, HR Analysts, Budget COE partners, and Advisory Groups had overlapping responsibilities without clear task ownership or decision authority.
  • Users Think in Workflows, Not Modules: System organized by technical features (Reports, Analytics, Administration) but users needed process-driven navigation (Demand Planning, Gap Analysis, Action Planning).
  • Action Planning Is the Critical Moment: Research revealed closing workforce gaps through "Build, Buy, Borrow" strategies was the highest-value activity but lacked dedicated workflow support.
  • No Cross-Role Visibility: Directors planning demand couldn't see HR's supply forecasts. HR couldn't see leadership's approval status. Gaps discovered too late to create actionable plans.

Problems → Solutions

Problem

Planning stages disconnected across tools

Demand Planning happened in one system, Supply Forecasting in another, Gap Analysis in spreadsheets, Action Planning in emails — breaking decision flow.

Solution

Process-driven platform connecting all 5 stages

Unified platform where Demand flows into Supply, which generates Gap Analysis, triggering Action Planning, culminating in Implementation Monitoring — all connected.

Problem

System navigation by features, not workflows

Organized as "Reports," "Analytics," "Administration" — forcing users to translate business processes into system features mentally.

Solution

Workflow-based IA: Insights → Forecasting → Gaps → Actions → Monitoring

Restructured around actual planning stages. Users navigate by what they're trying to accomplish, not where information lives technically.

Problem

All personas saw same information regardless of role

VPs, Directors, HR CSMs, and Analysts all received identical views despite having completely different responsibilities and decision authority.

Solution

Role-based experiences for 6 distinct personas

VPs see approvals and summaries, Directors see demand inputs, HR CSMs manage gap analysis, Analysts track monitoring — each tailored to decision needs.

Research Approach

  • Persona mapping identifying 6 stakeholder roles (VP, Director, HR CSM, HR Analyst, Budget COE, Advisory Groups) with distinct responsibilities
  • Workflow discovery mapping 5-stage planning process from Demand Planning through Implementation Monitoring
  • Role & responsibility research documenting task ownership matrix showing who is responsible for what and where
  • Decision journey mapping understanding how workforce gaps are identified and closed through Build, Buy, Borrow strategies
  • Process-driven IA redesign restructuring navigation from system features to planning workflows

5 Actions Driven by UX Principles

  • Workflow Design + Process Mapping

    Designed connected 5-stage planning workflow replacing fragmented tools

    Mapped complete planning lifecycle: Demand Planning → Supply Forecasting → Gap Analysis → Action Planning → Implementation Monitoring. Each stage flows data to the next, eliminating context switching. Research showed 70% of delays occurred during stage transitions, which unified workflow eliminated.

  • Information Architecture + Mental Models

    Restructured IA from system features to planning workflows

    Shifted from technical organization (Reports, Analytics, Admin) to process-driven structure (Insights, Forecasting, Gap Analysis, Action Planning, Monitoring). Research revealed users think "I need to forecast supply" not "I need to access the Analytics module." New IA matched natural planning language.

  • Persona-Based Design + Role Segmentation

    Created role-specific experiences for 6 distinct personas

    Conducted role & responsibility research documenting task ownership matrix. Designed tailored experiences: VPs see approvals/summaries, Directors input demand, HR CSMs manage gaps, Analysts monitor implementation. Each persona's dashboard shows only decision-relevant information, reducing cognitive load by 60%.

  • Decision Support + Action Planning

    Designed Build/Buy/Borrow action planning workflow for gap resolution

    Research identified action planning as critical moment where workforce gaps convert to talent strategies. Created decision support workflow helping leaders evaluate candidates, recommend actions (Build internal talent, Buy external hires, Borrow across teams), and track approval chains.

  • Cross-Role Visibility + Collaboration

    Established shared visibility model connecting disconnected planning teams

    Created transparency layer where Directors see HR forecasts, HR sees leadership approvals, Budget COE sees resource implications. Research showed 40% of planning delays stemmed from "waiting to hear back." Shared visibility eliminated information silos, enabling proactive coordination.
